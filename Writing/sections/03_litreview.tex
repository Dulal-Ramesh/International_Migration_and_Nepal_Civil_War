

\textbf{Context of Civil Conflict}\\
The roots of the conflict lay in long-standing political exclusion and regional inequality that democratic reforms in 1990 failed to address. Nepal's feudal legacy had concentrated land and political power among elites, leaving rural areas-particularly the mid-western hills-economically marginalized with limited access to education, healthcare and political representation.\\  
Various literature have discussed on the direct and indirect effect of violence on migration. Indirect effect relate to economic dislocation or economic turmoil causing people to migrate from their usual place of residence while the direct effect imply the threat of staying back rather than economic costs, push the households to move away. How does the violence impact migration- internal and external, differently is another issue.
