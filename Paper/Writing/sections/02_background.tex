The roots of the conflict lay in long-standing political exclusion and regional inequality that democratic reforms in 1990 failed to address. Nepal's feudal legacy had concentrated land and political power among elites, leaving rural areas-particularly the mid-western hills-economically marginalized with limited access to education, healthcare and political representation.\\
