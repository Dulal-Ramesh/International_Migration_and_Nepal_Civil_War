\label{sec:data}
\cite{Bohra-Mishra2011} The conflict intensity variable is defined on the basis of the month of war observed in a certain district. $months \, of \, war_{d}$ is defined as the total number of months, a district was exposed to conflict. This will be used as a baseline variable to represent the conflict intensity. This variable is constructed as below:
\begin{equation}
\text{conflict}_{d 1}
=\text{months of war}_{d}
=\sum_{m=1}^{131} 1(\text{casualty}_{d m}).
\label{eq:months_of_war}
\end{equation}
\[
1(\text{casualty}_{dm})=
\begin{cases}
1, & \text{if } \text{casualty}_{dm}>0,\\
0, & \text{otherwise.}
\end{cases}
\]
Subscripts $d$ and $m$ represent district and month of war. $m$ takes the value upto 131 due to the fact that the Maoist conflict reigned from February 1996 to November 2006 with total of 131 months.
