\label{sec:methods}

Following \cite{10.1257/aer.91.4.795}, we estimate:
\begin{equation}
Y_{idk} = c_{1} + \alpha_{d} + \beta_{k} + (P_d \times T_i)\gamma + \mathbf{X}_i' \delta  + \varepsilon_{idk}
\label{eq:did}
\end{equation}
where $Y_{idk}$ is an indicator for migration outcome for individual $i$, born in district $d$ and in year $k$. $\alpha_{d}$ is a district fixed effect, $\beta_{k}$ is a birth-year fixed effect, $T_i$ is an indicator variable for the child cohort exposed to conflict in their childhood (aged 0--17 at the beginning of conflict in 1996), and $P_d$ is an indicator for high conflict districts (above 75\textsuperscript{th} percentile of months of war from 1996--2006). $\mathbf{X}_i$ is a vector of individual-level controls including sex, ethnicity, education attainment. $\varepsilon_{idk}$ is a random, idiosyncratic error term, and standart errors are clustered at the district level. The coefficient $\gamma$ is our coefficient of interest which captures the effect of exposure to conflict in the childhood.

To examine how the effect of conflict exposure varies across cohorts, and to assess the parallel trends assumption underlying our identification, we generalize Equation~\ref{eq:did} to estimate age-specific coefficients:
\begin{equation}
Y_{idk} = c_{2} + \alpha_{d} + \beta_{k} + \sum_{\ell = 0}^{40}\bigl(P_d \times \mathbf{1}[A_i = \ell]\bigr)\gamma_{\ell} + \mathbf{X}_i' \delta + \varepsilon_{idk}
\label{eq:event_study}
\end{equation}
where $A_i$ denotes the age of individual $i$ when conflict started (1996) and $\mathbf{1}[\cdot]$ is the indicator function. The reference age is $r = 26$, the youngest age in the control cohort. Each coefficient $\gamma_{\ell}$ measures the effect of being in a high-conflict district for individuals aged $\ell$ at conflict onset, relative to the reference cohort.

This specification serves two purposes. First, it traces the dose-response curve across childhood ages ($\ell = 0, 1, \ldots, 17$), revealing whether the effect concentrates in any specific age. Second, the coefficients for placebo ages ($\ell = 27, 28, \ldots, 40$) provide a visual test of the parallel trends assumption.

Thus, in the main DiD specification, the control group is the adult cohort aged 26-40 at the beginning of conflict. In the event-study specification, these adult cohorts are shown separately by age and serve as placebo cohorts because they were already adults when the conflict began, their age-specific coefficients should be close to zero if childhood exposure is the relevant channel.
